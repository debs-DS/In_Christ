Chapter 1

verse 18

"The greater my wisdom, the greater my grief. To increase knowledge only increases sorrow"

The more Solomon understood, the more he learned, the more he realized he didn't know. The more wisdom he acquired about life, the more of life's sorrows he knew.
Solomon pursued human wisdom alone, not walking with God. There is no point in chasing after anything other than the Lord. He is reflecting on the long life he spent chasing after knowledge from the earth and not God.
This verse helps us understand that no matter what we do in life or whatever goals and dreams we decide to pursue, it is all useless if we do not turn to God and ask Him for guidance. As Christians, we are to look to God in whatever we do in order to accomplish anything and remain joyful at the end.

God bless us and may the Holy Spirit guide us and may we listen to his guidance.
In Jesus' Name
Amen

Chapter 3

verses 1-8

"For everything there is a season, a time for everything under heaven. A time to be born and a time to die. A time to plant and a time to harvest. A time to kill and a time to heal. A time to tear down and a time to build up. A time to cry and a time to laugh. A time to grieve and a time to dance. A time to scatter stones and a time to gather stones. A time to embrace and a time to turn away. A time to search and a time to quit searching. A time to keep and a time to throw away. A time to tear and a time to mend. A time to be quiet and a time to speak. A time to love and a time to hate. A time for war and a time for peace."

Solomon understood that though there are good things in life, the bad things can't be escaped. He describes life to have 'seasons', 'a time for everything'.
Unintentionally, as Christians, we tend to believe that just because we have God that bad things won't happen to us. On the contrary, because we have God bad things will happen to us and God will inevitably and miraculously get us through them.

Lord we have understood that there will be days of joy and days of sorrow. We pray, In Jesus' Name, that no matter what we feel, may it be happiness or sadness, that you will be with us, guide us, strengthen us and give us the wisdom to confide in you.
In Jesus' Name
Amen

Chapter 4

verse 4-6

"Then I observed that most people are motivated to success because they envy their neighbors. But this too, is meaningless - like chasing the wind. Fools fold their idle hands, leading them to ruin. And yet, Better to have one handful with quietness than two handfuls with hard work and chasing the wind."

"Those who put in hard work and succeed often get envied by those around them. Those who envy watches those who have succeeded and waste away because of their laziness and foolishness consumes them. Those who are content with the little they have or have earned are more at peace than those who constantly feel the need to succeed in anything and everything, "like chasing the wind"." - is what I believe is being said.

Holy Spirit please embed the spirit of contentment in us and destroy the spirit of laziness, foolishness, and envy from our hearts.
In Jesus' Name
Amen

verse 9-12

"Two people are better than one, for they can help each other succeed. If one person falls, the other can reach out and help. But someone eho falls alone is in real trouble. Likewise, two people lying close together can keep each other warm. But how can one be warm alone? A person standing alone can be attacked and defeated, but two can stand back-to-back and conquer. Three are even better, for a triple-braided cord is not easily broken."

What good is your success if you can't pass it on?
There is a man who has no friends, no family to care for. This man strives everyday to earn more and more. He has no one to pass his wealth to, no one to share his victories and failures with, yet he works so hard to gain so much. Solomon paints a picture of lonely, pointless busyness.
Without a friend, accomplishments are vain.
Solomon tells us that friendships and companionship brings productivity, comfort, safety and security
A triple-braided cord can represent the Trinity. A tight bond in which each person/individual in that bond complement each other's abilities.

Lord please send people to us that would act as true companions, that will pick us up when we fall and will complement our faults.
In Jesus' Name
Amen
